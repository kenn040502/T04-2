Televisions • Energy Insights

Audience: Households considering a new TV. These six charts (Q1–Q6) summarise key findings from the Energy Rating dataset. Each image is accompanied by a brief, plain-language takeaway.

Q1. Which TV screen technologies are most common?

Distribution of models by screen technology in the Australian market.

Takeaway: LED/LCD dominates; OLED, QLED and Mini-LED make up smaller shares.

Q2. What screen sizes are available and which are most frequent?

Frequency of models grouped into common diagonal size buckets.

Takeaway: 55″ models are typically most common; other sizes appear less often.

Q3. Which brands have the greatest number of models?

Count of distinct models by brand.

Takeaway: A few major brands lead in model variety; others contribute smaller ranges.

Q4. Which technology consumes the least power?

Average active power (W) across screen technologies.

Takeaway: LCD/LED often lowest on average; premium panels vary by model and features.

Q5. What is the relationship between screen size and power use?

Scatter of screen size (inches) vs average power (W).

Takeaway: Power tends to rise with size, though efficiency varies by brand/tech.

Q6. What is the relationship between star rating and screen size?

Distribution of sizes across star ratings (e.g., box plots).

Takeaway: High star ratings appear across popular sizes; efficiency depends on both size and technology.
